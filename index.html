I study agency problems of developing country governments at UChicago Harris. In particular, I examine how their organizational structure affects the market for corruption. More broadly, I am interested in the organizational and institutional determinants of bureaucratic state capacity. I hold a B.A. in Ethics, Politics and Economics from Yale University and have previously worked at Stanford FSE, UN ESCAP, and J-PAL Southeast Asia.
